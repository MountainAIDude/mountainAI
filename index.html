MOUNTAIN AI

1 GW off-grid supercluster · Colossus North Pitch

Alberta Cold Rush - Last Best West call for Engineers

Mountain AI is the idea of turning old mine and oil infra into a 1 gigawatt off-grid compute cluster in northern Alberta: stranded flare gas and geothermal co-production feeding high-density HPC and AI.

Mountain AI compute heat

Project Plan:

A cluster designed for 120 000 GPUs, built as a series of tightly coupled “heat blocks”. Each block power in from flare-derived gas and geothermal, compute in the middle, hot water and glycol out to nearby towns and industrial users.

Target is PUE 1.04 using the cold as an asset: −40 °C free air, ducted, backed by immersion and liquid loops wherever the density demands. Energy to sky instead as district heat, greenhouse heat, drying kilns, and process heat.

Ownership is designed from day one to include First Nations carried interest and revenue share, not as a late-stage ESG patch but as one of the core foundation stones. The land, the title path, and the long-term benefit have to make sense locally before any steel goes in.

Underground Infra

Underneath the pad in pipe runs, manifolds, conduit, and data paths. Hot water leaving the racks and traveling out along insulated mains. Return lines bringing cooled water back. Cables tying edge buildings, substations, turbines, and control rooms into a single nervous system.

The intent is simple to describe and hard to execute: treat high-density compute as a controllable heat source, schedule jobs and cooling as one problem, and spin up or down sections of the cluster to match heat demand without starving training runs.

On paper it is a 1 GW off-grid supercluster. In practice it is a bet that a cold region with under-used energy resources can host some of the cleanest, most efficient AI compute on earth, while literally heating homes.

Status: solo founder, pre-permit, pre-capital. Early stage, building the engineering story in public and looking for the people who know how to move pipe, steel, turbines, and governance in cold places.
